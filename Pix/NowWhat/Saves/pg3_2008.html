The next seven shots were taken just outside the building I work in in downtown Toronto on two different days. I was trying to achieve a certain combination of textures and mood. I had a mental picture of a group of pedestrians, essentially black figures from back-lighting, against the grey of a snowy city street with snow blowing in the air. This was to be rendered in a grainy meta-texture on matte paper. The underlying concept is grainy street photography with muted colours instead of the classic b&w.

This first attempt, probably comes closest, but lacks the blowing snow:

DC8-03

Update, Feb 24: having lived with 03 for a while, I made the following modifications: moved the woman on the left closer to the camera, cropped a bit on both sides to tight the composition, and brightened the central portion of the image.

DC8-03 revised

Moving the woman goes against the grain of everything street photographers hold sacred, since the picture no longer even pretends to record what the camera saw at a given moment. Sorry 'bout that; but I'm all about inner truth, not outer truth.
